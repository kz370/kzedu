2. Core Concepts: Beyond Buzzwords

Scalability vs. Performance

  • Performance: Speed of a single unit.
  • Scalability: Handling more load.

‚òï Analogy:
Performance: Training a barista to make espresso in 10s instead of 30s.
Scalability: Hiring 5 more baristas to serve 500 customers.

Load Balancing

Distributing traffic across multiple servers.

üëÆ Analogy: A traffic cop or receptionist directing a long line of people into 5 different open counters so no single counter gets overwhelmed.

CAP Theorem (Practical View)

Pick 2: Consistency, Availability, Partition Tolerance.

üó£Ô∏è Analogy: Two people on phones (Nodes) trying to agree on a meeting time (Data).
Partition (P): The call drops.
CP (Consistency): You stop talking because you can't confirm the time (System Errors out).
AP (Availability): You guess a time just to keep the conversation going, risking showing up at different times (Data Mismatch).

üìö Analogy: A library is too big for one room. You put books A-M in the East Wing (Server 1) and N-Z in the West Wing (Server 2).
Downside: If you need a book from A and a book from Z, you have to run between two rooms (Complex Transactions).

SQL (Relational)

Structured, Rigid.

üóÑÔ∏è Analogy: An Excel sheet where every row must have the exact same columns and data types.

Flexible, Fast.
